Soap Foam

Purpose / Question

The purpose of this experiment is to find out how different liquids affect the foaming of soap.

How would different liquids affect the foaming of soap?

Hypothesis

I believe that the olive oil will have the highest foam. Online it shows that they use different types oil to make Lather Clean and other types.

Procedures

1. Pour 1/8 of a Cup of water into a bottle or something to shake.
2. Pour 1/8 of a Cup of soap into the same container.
3. Pour 1/8 of a Cup of (your liquid) into the same container.
4. Shake the bottle or container three times.
5. Pour it into the Measuring Glass so you may measure or get a better look at it.
6. Repeat for EACH liquid to try out (Do NOT Use The Same Mixed Soap, This will produce inaccurate results, so start out fresh for each one)

Gain Soap

Pure 100% Lemon Juice

Olive Oil

Coconut Oil

Distilled Vinegar

Just Water

Distilled Vinegar

Lemon Juice

Coconut Oil

Results

My hypothesis was incorrect, it turns out that the Distilled Vinegar foamed the most. Olive Oil has actually ended up and one of the least ones to create foam, it created more of a thick foam.

Conclusion

My hypothesis was incorrect, the oil actually was the least foamy. The one that got the highest was distilled vinegar.
